# FSM GROUP

## Aviation Fuel Quality Control Manual - 2025 Edition

AFQC-40 - AIRLINE / CUSTOMER NOTIFICATION

November 2025 (Rev A)

## 40.1 Purpose

The purpose of this section is to establish the requirements and procedures for timely communication with airline customers and stakeholders whenever an event, test result, or condition may affect aviation fuel quality or operational safety. Effective notification ensures coordination, transparency, and rapid resolution in accordance with ATA Specification 103, Section 2.3.

## 40.2 Scope

This policy applies to all FSM-operated facilities and personnel engaged in the receipt, storage, transfer, or delivery of aviation fuel. It governs internal and external notifications involving fuel contamination, off-specification results, equipment failure, or other reportable events. Notifications shall also include any incident requiring carrier coordination or affecting fuel supply integrity.

## 40.3 Responsibilities

Responsibilities for implementing this policy are as follows:  
1\. \*\*Quality Control Personnel:\*\* Immediately report any test failure or abnormal result to the Quality Supervisor and initiate preliminary investigation.  
2\. \*\*Quality Supervisor:\*\* Evaluate findings, ensure confirmation testing, and notify the General Manager (GM) without delay.  
3\. \*\*General Manager:\*\* Oversee customer notification, ensure the affected product is quarantined, and verify proper documentation and follow-up.  
4\. \*\*Corporate Quality Assurance:\*\* Coordinate with the airline's quality representatives, provide technical support, and maintain central communication records.  
5\. \*\*Health, Safety, and Environmental (HSE) Department:\*\* Support notifications involving environmental or safety impacts.

## 40.4 Notification Procedure

All notifications shall follow a structured escalation process to ensure timely and accurate communication. The following steps apply whenever a fuel-related event or discrepancy is identified:

1\. QC personnel shall immediately isolate the affected system or product and inform supervision.  
2\. The Quality Supervisor shall confirm the result and notify the General Manager.  
3\. The General Manager shall contact the airline's local representative and Corporate QA.  
4\. Corporate QA shall notify all affected carriers and regulatory bodies as required.  
5\. A written summary, including corrective actions, shall be completed within 24 hours and filed per AFQC-710.  
6\. Verbal or informal communication must be documented using the Notification Log (Section 40.6).

## 40.5 Documentation and Recordkeeping

All notifications, reports, and follow-up correspondence must be documented in accordance with AFQC-710 (Digital Record Control Standard). Records shall include date/time of notification, personnel involved, method of communication, corrective actions, and closure date. Electronic copies must be stored in the FSM Quality Management System (QMS) and retained for a minimum of three years.
